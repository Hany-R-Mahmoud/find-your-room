# Research Brief

## Source Studied

- Proposal OCR: [`roommatch_ocr.txt`](/Users/hanyramadan/find-your-room/roommatch_ocr.txt)

## What The Proposal Gets Right

- There is a real trust and fragmentation problem in shared housing.
- Riyadh is a strong first-city wedge because of workforce concentration, affordability pressure, and frequent moves.
- Digital rental infrastructure in Saudi Arabia is materially better than a pure offline-market assumption would suggest.

## What Needed Correction

- The proposal is too broad for a first product.
- The claim that there is effectively no specialized competition is too strong.
- The `why now` rent-freeze angle is real, but it is recent and specific, not a timeless market truth.
- Hotels, chalets, serviced apartments, roommate matching, B2B housing, and smart-lock infrastructure do not belong in the same MVP.

## Current Facts We Validated

### Population and expat base

- Saudi Arabia's total population reached `35.3M` in 2024, with non-Saudis representing `44.4%` of the population.
- Source: [General Authority for Statistics, Population Estimates 2024](https://www.stats.gov.sa/documents/20117/2067012/Population%2BEstimates%2BStatistics%2B2024%2BEN.pdf/9b71e303-5fd9-19cb-9913-850a9d521639?t=1738859947691)

### Tourism and mobility signal

- In Saudi Ministry of Tourism material published in November 2024, the ministry stated Saudi Arabia welcomed `27M+` international tourists in 2023 and forecast `32M` incoming tourists in 2024.
- Source: [Ministry of Tourism hospitality investor report](https://cdn.mt.gov.sa/files/Hospitality-Investor.pdf)

### Rental contracts and digital rails

- Ejar stated in September 2024 that it had recorded `10M+` rental contracts since launch and that authenticated contracts in 2024 had already exceeded `1.5M`.
- Ejar also states that it verifies ownership documents and party identities through government integrations and uses `Mada` and `Sadad` payment rails.
- Source: [Ejar contract announcement, September 29, 2024](https://www.ejar.sa/ar/node/428143)

### Rent freeze context

- The Riyadh rent-freeze rule is real, but it began on `September 25, 2025`, not as a long-standing market condition.
- Source: [Ejar regulation update, September 25, 2025](https://www.ejar.sa/ar/event/431848)

### Hospitality licensing exists and is separate

- Ministry of Tourism service materials make clear that tourism accommodation is a licensed category with its own service flow and fee structure.
- That means hospitality inventory belongs to a different regulatory lane than a simple roommate or shared-room wedge.
- Source: [Ministry of Tourism hospitality facilities regulations service directory](https://cdn.mt.gov.sa/mtportal/mt-fe-production/content/policies-regulations/documents/services-directory/Hospitality-Facilities-Regulations-service-directory-En-V014.pdf)

### Competition already exists

- Specialized Saudi roommate and room-sharing products already exist, including Shreek Sakn, which currently markets itself as a room-and-roommate app across Saudi Arabia.
- Source: [Shreek Sakn](https://shreeksakn.com/en)

## Main Research Conclusion

RoomMatch should not launch as a broad `flexible living super app`.

The evidence supports a narrower wedge:

- Riyadh-first
- shared rooms and shared apartments first
- monthly stays first
- trust, verification, and compatibility first
- manual supply and compliance operations behind the scenes at launch

## Product Decision This Research Supports

`RoomMatch v1 = a verified Riyadh shared-housing marketplace with roommate-fit signals and trust workflows, not a hotel/chalet/B2B/smart-lock platform.`
