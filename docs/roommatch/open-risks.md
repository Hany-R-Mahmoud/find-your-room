# Open Risks

## Product Risks

- The original proposal still mixes multiple businesses.
- Users may interpret people-first matching as social or dating behavior unless the app language stays firmly in the housing lane.
- Matching quality will not matter if early inventory quality is weak.

## Business Risks

- A polished native app cannot compensate for weak verified supply.
- The deck's unit economics are not yet bottom-up enough to support investor confidence.
- Corporate housing and smart locks are attractive but likely premature.

## Regulatory And Operations Risks

- Shared housing, deposits, digital agreements, and short or mid-term stay rules need explicit operational validation.
- Hospitality inventory has a different licensing surface from simple shared-room matching.
- Host identity, listing legitimacy, and dispute handling need a concrete workflow before live launch.

## Technical Risks

- This repo currently represents a prototype, not a production-ready system.
- Backend choices, auth, payments, moderation, storage, and localization infrastructure are still open.

## Current Mitigation

- Narrow the product surface now.
- Build trust and household-fit UX first.
- Keep the first live supply loop partially manual.
- Defer high-regulation or hardware-heavy features until after liquidity proof.
